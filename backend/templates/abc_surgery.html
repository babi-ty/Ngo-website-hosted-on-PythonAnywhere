The ABC-AR Journey: From Vision to Law

1860s

The Failed Approach

Madras Corporation begins killing street dogs to control population. Despite killing increasing numbers annually, both dog population and human rabies cases continue to rise. Over a century of culling proves ineffective.

1964

A Revolutionary Idea is Born

Dr. S. Chinny Krishna proposes surgical sterilization as a humane alternative to killing. Blue Cross of India conceives and implements the world's first ABC-AR (Animal Birth Control - Anti Rabies) program for street dogs.

1990

Global Recognition

World Health Organization (WHO) officially endorses the ABC-AR methodology, recognizing it as an effective and humane approach to dog population management and rabies control.

1995-1996

Breakthrough in Chennai

After 30 years of advocacy, Corporation Commissioner S. Abul Hassan allows BCI to demonstrate ABC-AR in South Chennai. Within 5 months, visible results prompt the corporation to stop dog killing city-wide in September 1996.

1997

National Policy

Animal Welfare Board of India (AWBI) adopts ABC-AR, making it a national policy. Radford University cites it as one of the watershed moments in world environmental history.

2001

Legal Requirement

Government of India notifies 'Animal Birth Control (Dogs) Rules', making ABC-AR a legal requirement throughout India. This marks the complete transformation from killing to humane management.

2007

Zero Rabies Deaths

Chennai records ZERO human deaths due to rabies for the first time since records were kept. Human rabies cases dropped from 120 in 1996 to zero, directly attributed to aggressive ABC-AR implementation.

Present

Global Model

Blue Cross of India has trained organizations worldwide. Cities across India and globally have adopted ABC-AR, saving millions of animals while effectively controlling rabies.

How ABC-AR Works

1

CATCH

Trained teams humanely catch street dogs using specialized equipment, ensuring minimal stress to the animals.

2

STERILIZE

Qualified veterinarians perform spay/neuter surgeries in fully-equipped operation theaters under proper anesthesia.

3

VACCINATE

All animals receive anti-rabies vaccination during the procedure, protecting both animal and human populations.

4

RETURN

After recovery, animals are released at the exact GPS location where they were picked up, with ear notch identification.

The Chennai Success Story

From Crisis to Zero: The Rabies Revolution

120 Human Rabies Deaths (1996)
0 Human Rabies Deaths (2007)

After decades of failed culling policies, Chennai's transformation through ABC-AR demonstrates the program's effectiveness beyond doubt.

The turning point: In 1995, BCI was allowed to pilot ABC-AR in South Chennai. Commissioner S. Abul Hassan personally monitored results. Within 5 months, every street dog had a notched ear marking sterilization and vaccination.

The result: The city immediately stopped its century-old dog killing program. Over the next decade, human rabies deaths plummeted from 120 annually to zero, while street dog behavior improved dramatically.

Global impact: This success inspired cities across India and worldwide to abandon cruel and ineffective culling in favor of humane, scientific ABC-AR programs.
